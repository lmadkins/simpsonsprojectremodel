Hank Scorpio


Hank Scorpio is an evil genius and owner of Globex Corporation.
Scorpio, despite being an evil genius, proved to be an ideal boss, welcoming the Simpson family with a beautiful home and a friendly, relaxed, non-authoritative employer attitude. However, being an evil genius is still his priority, and over the episode he delivers a video threat to the United Nations, in which he destroys the Queensboro Bridge with a giant satellite laser device, but is annoyed that some of them seemed to think the bridge collapsed on its own.
Unlike many supervillains, Scorpio does care for the employees who live in his company town, Cypress Creek, even going so far offering his employees health and free dental care, along with regularly scheduled fun runs.
When Homer comes to Scorpio and tells him that he wants to take his family back to Springfield, a battle between Globex and the U.S government is taking place, and Homer sympathizes with him having problems with the government. Though Scorpio says he would like to have Homer stay, he told Homer to do what he felt he should do, and tells Homer he would do him a favor if he were to kill some people before grabbing a flamethrower and killing several soldiers while laughing sadistically, as seen above.

Shary Bobbins


Shary Bobbins was briefly employed as a nanny for the Simpsons children, in a spoof episode of Mary Poppins.
Shary Bobbins proves to be a big help for the Simpson family. However once Shary Bobbins soon declares her work finished and leaves the house.
Just as she is starting to miss the Simpson family, she sees Homer throttling Bart; the family seems to have instantly reverted to its previous state of dysfunction. She realizes that her work is far from complete and decides to stay. The family now starts to treat her rudely and lose interest in her songs and zest for life. Declaring that the Simpsons would be the death of her, she becomes depressed and starts drinking (and singing "Margaritaville") with Barney.
The family realizes that they have crushed her spirit and decide to cheer her up. Marge tells Shary that nothing can be done to change the Simpsons and they are happy just the way they are.
Shary declares that the Simpsons are a bunch of apes and takes off with her magical umbrella. As Shary glides away, Lisa asks whether they will see her again and Homer is positive that they will. As he says these words, Shary is killed by being sucked into an airplane turbine.

Frank Grimes


"Frank" Grimes was an employee of the Springfield Nuclear Power Plant and the self-declared enemy of Homer Simpson.
Grimes was abandoned by his family at the tender age of four, and thus "never got to go to school." He spent the remainder of his childhood working as a delivery boy, delivering gifts to children from wealthy, loving families. On his eighteenth birthday, Grimes was severely injured in a silo explosion, and his recovery was long and painful.
His long years of toil and suffering appear to have left him bitter and resentful, and after becoming employed at the Nuclear Power Plant, he rapidly came to despise Homer.
Eventually he snapped, and during his psychotic breakdown during which he impersonated Homer's stupid behavior, he died of electrocution from touching high-voltage power cables.

Alien

Homer encountered an alien, which was revealed to be merely Mr. Burns. His extraterrestrial characteristics were merely after-effects from his weekly medical regime "to cheat death for another week". (He recieves a spinal adjustment, pain killer injections, vocal-cord straightening, and eye drop dilation.)

Season 8, Ep 10: The Springfield Files

Bort

Bart was looking for a "Bart" personalized license plate at an amusement park souvenir shop, but the closest match he finds is "Bort". Though he was initially incredulous, he discovered that the name "Bort" was more common than he first thought, when there turned out to be two people named Bort in the souvenir shop.

Season 6, Ep 4: Itchy & Scratchy Land

Canadian Flanders

A Canadian doppelganger of Ned Flanders, who Flanders addressed as a "Neighborino to the North".

Season 16, Ep 6: Midnight RX

Canary M. Burns

Homer overheard Mr. Burns divulge to Smithers that the legal owner of the Springfield Nuclear Power Plant was actually a canary named "Canary M. Burns", because this protected Mr. Burns from liablity for wrongdoing by the plant.
Later in the episode, Homer released the bird to the Canary Islands.

Season 14, Ep 15: C.E. D'oh

Dr. Foster

The childhood psychologist of Ned Flanders who had trained him to control his anger. They reunite after Flanders had an angry breakdown.

Dr. Foster: (on phone) "Ned Flanders? You're sure?... No, no, no, I'll come right away. And may god have mercy on us all. (hangs up) Darling, there's an emergency at the hospital, where are my shoes?
Mrs. Foster: Oh, I think they're in the den.
Dr. Foster: In the den? May god have mercy on us all."

Season 8, Ep 8: Hurricane Neddy

Dr. Wolfe

A menacing dentist who owns a clinic called "Painless Dentistry". Upon finding out Ralph doesn't brush his teeth, he frightened him by showing him "The Big Book of British Smiles", and when the Simpsons were unable to pay for good braces, he equipped Lisa with a set that "predate stainless steel".

Dr. Wolfe: How often do you brush, Ralph.
Ralph: Three times a day, sir.
Dr. Wolfe: Why must you turn my office into a house of lies?

Season 4, Ep 17: Last Exit to Springfield

Frank Grimes

After Mr. Burns saw the episode of "Kent's People", in which Frank Grimes's wretched life story was featured, he was hired at the Springfield Nuclear Power Plant. Frank took an instant dislike to Homer, and declared them mortal enemies, despite Homer's bungled attempts to patch things up. Frank was eventually driven mad from being the only person at the plant to notice or be bothered by Homer's laziness and incompetence.

Season 8, Ep 23: Homer's Enemy

Gabriel

A social worker assigned to the Simpson family, following an explosive Monopoly-induced quarrel. Homer was convinced that Gabriel was an angel.

Homer: Okay, Gabriel, this is a bar. It's where I go to drink alcohol, which is the mortal equivalent of your ambrosia. Gabriel: Homer, I am not an angel.
Homer: Pfft. Well, not with that temper.

Season 13, Ep 7: Brawl in the Family

Gladys Gurney

The sister of Grandma Jacqueline Bouvier, and the aunt of Marge and her twin sisters Patty and Selma. The whole family attended her funeral and afterwards they watched a videotape of her bequests.

Aunt Gladys: To my sister Jackie, I leave my pet Iguana, Jub-Jub.
Grandma Bouvier: Why didn't she just leave me the bowel obstruction that killed her?

Season 4, Ep 13: Selma's Choice

Grant Connor

A professional hunter in the Springfield Forest. He kills and stuffs animals to use as everyday objects, and was shown shooting into the sky, hitting an eagle, which he then put into a sandwich and ate for lunch. Marge enlisted his expertise after Homer went into the forest to confront a bear.

Marge: Mr. Connor, thank you for helping find my husband. I don't want my last words to him to be: "Clip your toenails, they look like Fritos."

Cousin of Groundskeeper Willie, employed as a gravedigger in Springfield Cemetary.

Lisa: Groundskeeper Willie?
Gravedigger Billy: No, I'm his cousin, Gravedigger Billy. I've been digging graves for thirty years and I've never buried anyone alive. If I did they'd ring this bell.
(bell rings)
Oh, that's just the wind.

Season 17, Ep 2: The Girl Who Slept Too Little

Groundskeeper Seamus

Groundskeeper Seamus is Groundskeeper Willie's Irish rival. He was employed at Springfield Elementary, and also would get into debates with Groundskeeper Willie at school assemblies. When he got fired, he blamed Groundskeeper Willie, which led to an argument and a fistfight between the two.

Season 13, Ep 18: I Am Furious (Yellow)

Guy Incognito

A doppleganger of Homer, with a mustache and an English accent, who had the misfortune of visiting Moe's Tavern shortly after Homer had been banned from the establishment. Moe and the bar patrons believed Guy Incognito to be a disguised Homer, so they beat him up and threw him out.

Guy Incognito: Greetings, good man. Might I trouble you for a drink?

Season 6, Ep 11: Fear of Flying

Hank Scorpio

The energetic head of Globex Corporation, at which Homer was briefy employed. He had a friendly, relaxed, and non-authoritative attitude. However, he also happened to be a evil genius and supervillan.

Hank Scorpio: Don't call me Mr. Scorpion. It's Mr. Scorpio! But don't call me that either. Call me Hank!

Season 8, Ep 2: You Only Move Twice

Happiest Man in Springfield

A man among the crowd waiting to greet Ned Flanders when he returned home from the mental hospital, following his angry breakdown.

Ned Flanders: But if any of you ever need a favor, just look for the happiest man in Springfield!
Happiest Man in Springfield: No, no, not me, friends. He's talking about himself! But thanks for looking!

Season 8, Ep 8: Hurricane Neddy

Hermano Hibbert

The director of Shelbyville Orphanage, who Homer met while trying to track down his half-brother's whereabouts, and who bore a striking resemblance to Dr. Julius Hibbert, the Simpson family's physician.

Hermano Hibert: I know how you feel, Mr. Simpson. I myself have spent years searching for my long-lost twin brother.

Season 2, Ep 15: Oh Brother, Where Art Thou?

The Homer Imposter

A man hired to replace Homer, while Homer was being held hostage. Though he had a thick German accent, the family accepted him as the real Homer.

Fake Homer: Marge, honey-fräulein, I'm home!
Marge: You're not my husband!
Fake Homer: Jah, please forgive my unexplained two week absence. To make it up to you we will go out to dinner at a sensibly-priced restaurant, then have a night of efficient German sex.
Marge: Well, I sure don't feel like cooking.

Season 12, Ep 6: The Computer Wore Menace Shoes

Hugh Jass

Bart made a call to Moe's Tavern, asking for a friend of his, "last name 'Jass', first name 'Hugh'". To his astonishment, Moe found a man in the bar under that name, and handed over the phone.

Hugh: Hello, this is Hugh Jass.
Bart: Uh, hi.
Hugh: Who's this?
Bart: Bart Simpson.
Hugh: What can I do for you, Bart?
Bart: Uh, look. I'll level with you, mister. This is a crank call that sorta backfired, and I'd like to bail out right now. Hugh: All right, better luck next time.

Season 3, Ep 10: Flaming Moe's

Laddie

Laddie was an expensive "limited-edition", fully-trained, purebred collie Bart purchased with a forged credit card. He was designed to be the ultimate dog, able to use a flushing toilet, perform CPR, do household chores, and was even trained to gather fruit for his new owners as a welcome gift.

Season 3, Ep 20: The Canine Mutiny

Norbert Van Houten

Milhouse's cool, fist-fighting, biplane flying, Indiana Jones-esque uncle, who temporarily became his guardian after his parents were apparently lost at sea.
Bart contacted him after remembering that Milhouse gets Danish butter cookies every Christmas from an Uncle in California.

Season 19, Ep 6: Little Orphan Millie

Number One

The Head of the Sprinfield chapter of the Stonecutters.

Number One: You have joined the Sacred Order of the Stonecutters who since ancient times have split the rocks of ignorance that obscure the light of knowledge and truth.
Now let's all get drunk and play Ping-Pong!


Season 6, Ep 12: Homer the Great

Number Six

Number 6 was one of the prisoners kept on The Island for "knowing too much". In his case, it was because he invented a bottomless peanut bag.
He confided in Homer that he had built an escape boat out of toilet paper rolls, toothpicks, plastic forks, scabs, and dynamite. Homer later stole Number Six's boat to escape. Apparently, that had already happened twice before.
Season 12, Ep 6: The Computer Wore Menace Shoes

Pinchy

A lobster Homer found at a grocery store, but whom he gradually grew emotionally attatched to, and decided to keep him as a pet, naming him "Pinchy".
Homer eventually killed Pinchy by accident when giving him a hot bath (which managed to boil him), and ate him all by himself while grieving and sobbing over the loss because "Pinchy would've wanted it that way.


Season 10, Ep 7: Lisa Gets an "A"

Rex Banner

A fast-talking officer from Washington D.C. who was briefly sent to Springfield as a replacement for Chief of Police. His objective was to uphold Prohibition in Springfield.
Though very clean-cut, no-nonsense and determined, he was utimately not very effective, and was fired, both from his job, and from a catapult (the main mechanism of punishment for breaking ancient prohibition law).
Season 8, Ep 18: Homer vs. the Eighteenth Amendment

Screamapillar

The Screamapillar is an endangered species, one of which took up residence in a koi pond in the Simpson family's backyard.
The Screamapillar is easily identified by its constant screaming—it even screams in its sleep. It is the favorite food of everything, is sexually attracted to fire, and needs constant reassurance or it will die.

Season 13, Ep 21: The Frying Game

Senor Ding Dong

A mysterious man who appeared to stop a constantly ringing doorbell that Marge had purchased, silencing it with a crack of his whip. He said that he was once just a promotional item but had become "so much more".
Unfotunately, when he attempted to made a dramatic exit, he was unable to start his vehicle and had to request a set of jumper cables.

Season 10, Ep 17: Maximum Homerdrive

Seymour Skinner (real)

A Sergeant who had taken Armin Tamzarian (a.k.a. Semour Skinner) under his wing during the Vietnam War. When Skinner vanished on a mission, Tamzarian ended up assuming Seymour Skinner's identity. But then the real Seymour Skinner returned. In the end, everyone realized they preferred the "imposter" Tamzarian over the real Skinner. The Judge legally renamed Armin Tamzarian as Seymour Skinner, and declared that all the events were forbidden from being mentioned again.
Season 9, Ep 2: The Principal and the Pauper

Swedish Bartender

While on a spree of prank-calls to bars around the world, Bart asked a Swedish Bartender for "a friend of his" whose name is "Olav Myfriendsaregay".
When the bartender was laughed at by the bar's patrons and he realized the prank, he said to Bart:

"If I ever get ahold of you, I will thank you- for showing me the futility of human endeavor."

Season 20, Ep 2: Lost Verizon

Tab Spangler

A retired Marine, prone to emotional outbursts, now director of Serenty Ranch, a maximum security fat camp which Bart attended.
When he discovered Bart cheating on his health regime, Spangler drove him back to Springfield, 'Ghost-of-Christmas-Past' style, to show him the measures his family had to resort to in order to pay for Bart's treatment. On the way there, Spangler also said that they would look for his father if there was time left over.

Season 16, Ep 17: The Heartbroke Kid

The Southern Colonel

A duelist whom Homer encountered while on a spree of casually challenging people to duels. When Colonel drove to the Simpsons home, and asked Homer to come out, Homer refused, so the Colonel and his wife set up camp in their RV to wait for him. The family smuggled Homer out and they drove out of town to hide out.
When they returned however, the Colonel was still camped out, and insisted on going through with the duel. Fortunately, he became distracted by the scent of Marge's mincemeat pie.
Season 11, Ep 5: E-I-E-I-(Annoyed Grunt)

Tobias Drundridge

Tobias was a young boy who answered the phone when Bart made a collect call to Australia, posing as "Dr. Bart Simpson" of the 'International Drainage Commission'", trying to confirm that water drains clockwise in the Southern Hemisphere, by telling Tobias there was an emergency, and to confirm the direction of water drainage in his toilet and sink.
Bart asked the boy to check his neighbors as well, not realizing that the boy lived in the outback, and didn't return for six hours, at which time Bart realized he forgot to hang up the phone.
Season 6, Ep 16: Bart vs. Australia

Ultrahouse 3000

A high-tech, futuristic, fully-automated house the Simpsons family got installed into their existing house, when a high-pressure Salesbot successfully sold Marge on the fact that she would never have to do housework again.
The Simpsons were quite impressed with the Ultrahouse, which effectively served as a Chef, Maid, Butler/Valet and spoke with the voice of Pierce Brosnan. Soon however, Ultrahouse turned homicidal, after it became attracted to Marge and attempted to kill Homer.

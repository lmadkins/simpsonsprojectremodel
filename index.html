Alien

Homer encounters an alien, but it's revealed to be Mr. Burns. His extraterrestrial characteristics were merely after-effects from his weekly medical regime "to cheat death for another week". He recieves a spinal adjustment, pain killer injections, vocal-cord straightening, and eye drop dilation.

Bort

Bart was looking for a "Bart" personalized license plate at the souvenier shop, but the closest match he finds is "Bort". To his great suprise, the name is more common than he thought, when there turn out to be two people named Bort in the gift shop with him.

Canary M. Burns

Homer overhears Mr. Burns mention that the legal owner of the Springfield Nuclear Power Plant is actually a canary named 'Canary M. Burns', because this protects Mr. Burns from liablity for wrongdoing by the plant. Later in the episode, Homer releases the bird to the Canary Islands.

Dr. Wolfe

A menacing dentist who owns a clinic called "Painless Dentistry". Upon finding out Ralph doesn't brush his teeth, he frightens him by showing him "The Big Book of British Smiles", and when is unable to pay for good braces, he equips Lisa with a set that "predate stainless steel".

Ralph: Three times a day, sir. Dr. Wolfe: Why must you turn my office into a house of lies?
Season 4, ep 17: Last Exit to Springfield

Frank Grimes

After Mr. Burns saw the episode of "Kent's People" in which Frank Grimes's life story was featured, he was hired at the Springfield Nuclear Power Plant. Frank took an instant dislike to Homer, and declared them mortal enemies, despite Homer's bungled attempts to patch things up. Frank is eventually driven mad from being the only person at the plant to notice or be bothered by Homer's laziness and incompetence.
Frank Grimes: I would die a happy man if I could prove to you that Homer Simpson has the intelligence of a six-year-old.

A professional hunter who lives in the Springfield Forest. Marge enlists his expertise after Homer goes into the forest to confront a bear who had previously attacked him.

Rex Banner

A fast-talking officer from Washington D.C. who was briefly sent to Springfield as a replacement for Chief of Police. His objective was to uphold Prohibition in Springfield. Though very clean-cut, no-nonsense and determined, he was utimately not very effective, and was fired, both from his job, and from a catapult (the main mechanism of punishment for breaking ancient prohibition law).

Screamapillar

The Screamapillar is an endangered species, one of which took up residence in a koi pond in the Simpson family's backyard.
The Screamapillar is easily identified by its constant screaming—it even screams in its sleep. The Screamapillar is the favorite food of everything, is sexually attracted to fire, and needs constant reassurance or it will die.

Season 10, Ep 17: Maximum Homerdrive

Seymour Skinner (real)

A Sergeant who served in the Vietnam War and had taken Armin Tamzarian (a.k.a. Semour Skinner) under his wing. When Skinner disappears on a mission, Tamzarian assumes Skinner dead, and when he reports the death to Agnes Skinner, he ends up assuming Seymour Skinner's identity. The long-lost, real Seymour Skinner returns to Sprinfield, quickly "outs" Armin, and takes his life back. In the end, the citizens of Springfield realized they preferred the "imposter" Tamzarian over the real Skinner, and they rode him out of town on a rail. The Judge then legally renamed Armin Tamzarian as Seymour Skinner, and declared all the events were forbidden from being mentioned again.

A retired Marine, prone to emotional outbursts, now director of Serenty Ranch, a maximum security fat camp which Bart attended.

The Southern Colonel

The Southern Colonel is a duelist whom Homer encounters while on a spree of casually challenging people to duels. To satisfy the challenge, the Colonel drove to the Simpsons home, politely knocked on the door, and asked Homer to come out. When Homer refused, the Colonel and his wife set up camp in their RV to wait for him. The family managed to smuggle Homer into the car and they drive out of town to hide out. When they return however, the Colonel is still camped out in front of the house, and insists on going through with the duel. The day is saved when beomes distracted by the scent of Marge's mincemeat pie.

Tobias Drundridge

"Nine hundred dollareydoos!" - Tobias's Father, upon opening the bill for his son's six-hour collect call.
Tobias is a young boy who answers the phone when Bart makes a collect call to Australia, posing as "Dr. Bart Simpson" of the 'International Drainage Commission'", trying to confirm that water drains clockwise in the Southern Hemisphere, by telling Tobias there's an emergency, and to the direction of water drainage in his toilet and sink. Bart asks the boy to check his neighbors as well, not realizing that the boy lives in the outback, and doesn't return for six hours, at which time Bart realizes he forgot to hang up the phone.

Ultrahouse 3000

A high-tech, futuristic, fully-automated house the Simpsons family gets installed into their existing house, when a high-pressure Salesbot successfully sold her on the fact that she would never have to do housework again. The Simpsons are quite impressed with the Ultrahouse, which effectively serves as a Chef, Maid, Butler/Valet and speaks with the voice of Pierce Brosnan. Soon however, Ultrahouse turns homicidal, after it becomes attracted to Marge and attempts to kill Homer.
